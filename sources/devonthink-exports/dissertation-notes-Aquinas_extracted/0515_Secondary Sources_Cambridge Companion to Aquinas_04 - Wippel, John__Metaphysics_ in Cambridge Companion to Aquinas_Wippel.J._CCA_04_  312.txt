# Wippel.J._CCA_04:  312

Aquinas notes that if something is to be the subject of an accident, it must participate in esse (or, as he had previously phrased it, in the act of being).17 In other words, it must exist. And subsequently, in commenting on two other Boethian axioms, Aquinas concludes that just as existence (esse) and "that which is" differ intentionally (notionally) in the case of simple beings, they differ really in the case of composites.
