As we have seen, the proper objects of human cognition are the forms existing in material things, which the intellect abstracts from phantasmata. Since these are the things we first know, our language was constructed to speak of material things and their attributes.51 And so for any predicate which denotes a positive perfection, the predicate denotes the perfection as existing in a creaturely manner, and hence a manner which is not absolutely perfect.
