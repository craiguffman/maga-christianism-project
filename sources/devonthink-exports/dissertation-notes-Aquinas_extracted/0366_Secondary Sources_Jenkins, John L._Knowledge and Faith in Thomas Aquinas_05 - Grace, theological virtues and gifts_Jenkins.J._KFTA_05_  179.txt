# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  179

Returning to curative grace, Aquinas regularly distinguishes two sorts: "grace can be understood in either of two ways: in one way, [it is] the divine help by which [God] moves us to will and act well; in another way, it is the habitual gift divinely implanted in us."74 The first we will call actual grace; it consists in a certain actualization of some potency by God, as when God moves the will of an evil person to desire good. The second we will call habitual grace; it consists in the infusion of virtuous habits.
