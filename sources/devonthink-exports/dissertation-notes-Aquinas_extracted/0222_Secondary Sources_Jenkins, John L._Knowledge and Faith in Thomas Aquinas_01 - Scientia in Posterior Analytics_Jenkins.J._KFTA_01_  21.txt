There are also two scientiae which are exceptional in that they do not treat a particular genus, and have special relationships to the common principles. First philosophy or metaphysics "is about what is common, because its examination is of common things, namely of being and the parts or attributes of being."55 Since it considers all being, it is not restricted to a particular genus of being, but considers all things insofar as they are. It uses the common principles as its proper principles and moves from these to establish the proper principles of the particular scientiae.56 The second exception is dialectic, which argues from common logical notions to establish 57 what belongs to one or another particular scientia.Read more at location 423
