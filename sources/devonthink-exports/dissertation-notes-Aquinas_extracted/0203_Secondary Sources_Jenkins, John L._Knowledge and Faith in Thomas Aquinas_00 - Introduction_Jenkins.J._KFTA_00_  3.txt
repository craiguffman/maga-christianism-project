A noteworthy feature of this account, however, is that a condition for perfect scientia of some predication is that not only must one know the cause of this predication being true (in the Aristotelian sense of formal, material, efficient or final cause), but one must also know the cause better than its effect. The reason for this rather stringent requirement is that, for perfect scientia, one's awareness of the cause must eventually become the cause of one's awareness of the effect.
