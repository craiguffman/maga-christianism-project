# Jenkins.J._KFTA_04:  113

When Aquinas distinguishes the first and second operations of the intellect, he is using the word intellect (intellectus) in a more general sense than he uses it elsewhere. In this sense, it is used "commonly" to refer to all cognitive operations,15 in something like the way the English word mind is used. However, Aquinas also uses intellectus in a more specific sense - which is its original and more proper sense - to refer to the first operation alone. Using the word in this sense, Aquinas says "the proper object of the intellect is the quiddity of a thing.""
