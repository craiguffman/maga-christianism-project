one who falls well short of perfect apprehension of the principles of any scientia can nevertheless be said to have acquired scientia of the field imperfectly. But this is particularly so in the case of sacred doctrine. For although its principles are most knowable in themselves, they are least knowable to us. Unlike other scientiae, then, one must rely on the guidance and instruction of a teacher for the principles. In this case the teacher is God, and through believing the articles of faith as revealed by God, and through guidance by the Holy Spirit, one relies on God as a master in the acquisition of this scientia.
