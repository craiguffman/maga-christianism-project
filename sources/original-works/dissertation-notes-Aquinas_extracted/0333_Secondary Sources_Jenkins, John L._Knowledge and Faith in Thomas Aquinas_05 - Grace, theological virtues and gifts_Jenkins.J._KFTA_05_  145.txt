# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  145

When God acts within the UNO-natural operations of creatures, He acts within the natural order which He established at creation. He can and does however act outside this order, as when He parted the Red Sea, healed the man born blind and, indeed, when He gives supernatural grace. And all creatures have what Aquinas calls an obediential potency (potentia obedientialis, or sometimes potentia obedien- tiae) to be moved in this way. This notion of obediential potency is critically important in Aquinas's account of grace, and as prelude to our discussion of grace we turn now to Aquinas's notion of obediential potency.
