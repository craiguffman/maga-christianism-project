# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  141

The end or good proper to each creature is that which is attainable through the exercise of its potencies. God has ordered all creation, though, to attain a universal good, which is the divine end, the profusion of divine goodness. Just as an axe cannot carve a figure in stone without the skill of the artisan, so a creature cannot play a role in attaining the universal good without God's ordering of creation.Read more at location 1926
