# Jenkins.J._KFTA_02:  54

As we have seen, the PA describes a paradigmatic notion of scientia, scientia simpliciter, which fully and perfectly fulfills the conditions for being a scientia, and other sorts of non-paradigmatic scientiae fulfill these conditions in a partial or less perfect way. One could say, then, that in the PA we find an ordered set of notions of scientia, which consists of the paradigmatic case and others which more or less perfectly approximate to the paradigm. The advocate of the identity thesis claims that the notion of scientia in Aquinas's Summa theologiae falls somewhere in the spectrum of notions of scientia described in the PA.
