# Jenkins.J._KFTA_06:  253

An interesting view of the epistemic evaluation of the assent of faith, or of any voluntary assent, seems implicit in Aquinas's account. He seems to imply that moral virtues and vices, which enable us to act well or poorly in non-epistemic matters, also enable one to act well or poorly in attaining the epistemic goal of believing truths and disbelieving falsehoods. In other words, for Aquinas the moral virtues and vices are at the same time epistemic virtues and vices.
