# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  139

God is the final cause of my actions not only when I act for what is genuinely good, but even when I act for what is only an apparent good;` for even then one seeks the good as good. And, indeed, in this sense God is the final cause not only of human agents, but of all creatures. For all creatures, whether cognizant or not, can be said to tend toward the good as good when they seek their proper natural end, and thus they can be said to tend toward goodness itself, which is God. In this sense, God is the final cause of the natural operations not only of cognizant creatures, but of all creatures.Read more at location 1907
