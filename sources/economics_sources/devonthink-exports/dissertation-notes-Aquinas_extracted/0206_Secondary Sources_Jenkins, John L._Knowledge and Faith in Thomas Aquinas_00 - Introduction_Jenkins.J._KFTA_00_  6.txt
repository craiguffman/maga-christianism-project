# Jenkins.J._KFTA_00:  6

The structure of the scientia of the Summa theologiae, sacred doctrine or Christian theology, differs from other forms of scientia which humans can have, for in it humans participate in God's own scientia. The principles of this scientia are the articles of faith which have been revealed by God and accepted in faith. Although these principles cannot be fully understood and must remain mysteries in this life, and although this scientia transcends many of the limitations to which other scientiae are subject, sacred doctrine is properly a scientia subject to the fundamental conditions of an Aristotelian scientia.
