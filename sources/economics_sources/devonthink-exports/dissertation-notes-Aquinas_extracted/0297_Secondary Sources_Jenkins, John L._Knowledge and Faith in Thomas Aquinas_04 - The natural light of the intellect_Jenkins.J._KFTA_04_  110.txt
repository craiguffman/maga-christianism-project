# Jenkins.J._KFTA_04:  110

The potency or faculty by which essences, and hence definitions, are grasped is the intellect, which Aquinas calls the "habit of principles." He writes: We are properly said to understand (intelligere) when we apprehend the quiddity of things, or when we understand these things which are immediately known by the intellect once it knows the quiddities of things, such as the first principles (which we apprehend once we apprehend the terms). Hence intellect is called the habit of principles.5 Indeed, because the intellect is that by which we grasp principles, and a scientia is virtually contained in its principles, the intellect is called the "principium scientiae", the principle of scientia.
