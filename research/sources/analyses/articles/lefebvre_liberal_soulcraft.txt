## 1. Source Metadata
- **Title**: Liberal Soulcraft
- **Author(s)**: Alexandre Lefebvre
- **Publication**: Persuasion
- **Year**: 2025 (appears to be recent)
- **Type**: Article
- **Primary Discipline**: Political Philosophy

## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: Liberalism's future depends on citizens living up to its values in practice, not just promoting them rhetorically, to restore faith in liberalism as a fair system.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - "Liberaldom" (the hypocritical, self-satisfied cousin of liberalism) is unstable
  - Moralism combined with hypocrisy breeds resentment
  - The way forward is for liberals to embody liberal values in their personal lives
  - Liberalism needs to be lived, not just theorized, to be effective and legitimate
- **Methodology**: Philosophical reflection with cultural criticism
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to discussions about the crisis of liberalism and potential paths forward
