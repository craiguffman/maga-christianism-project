## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: The historical treatment of Japanese Americans during WWII and South Asian immigrants in 1923 reveals America's struggle with racism and citizenship, demonstrating how exclusionary policies based on race have shaped American history.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  1. Executive Order 9066 led to the unjust imprisonment of Japanese Americans, showing how war can intensify racial discrimination
  2. The 1923 Thind Supreme Court case exemplifies how citizenship was restricted by racial categories
  3. Legal discrimination against Asian immigrants has deep historical roots in American policy
  4. These historical injustices eventually led to legal reforms, showing the gradual evolution of American concepts of citizenship
- **Methodology**: Historical narrative that connects specific events (Executive Order 9066, Thind case) to broader patterns of racial discrimination and citizenship rights
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to scholarship on the history of American citizenship, immigration policy, and racial discrimination
