## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: Digital gardens represent an alternative approach to online content that emphasizes contextual knowledge, personal curation, and ongoing growth rather than time-bound streams.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - Digital gardens reject the chronological, stream-based organization of most online platforms
  - Gardens emphasize context, personal curation, and ongoing evolution
  - They make the knowledge development process visible rather than only showing finished products
  - Gardens embrace imperfection and epistemological transparency
  - They allow for multimedia expression and deep contextual understanding
- **Methodology**: Conceptual analysis with historical context and practical examples
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to discussions about alternative digital knowledge structures beyond social media
