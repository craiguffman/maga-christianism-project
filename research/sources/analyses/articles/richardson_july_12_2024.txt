## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: Representative Glenn Grothman's comments about returning to 1960s gender roles reflect a long-standing reactionary strategy of framing equality for women and minorities as socialism and government overreach.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - A Republican congressman explicitly advocated for rolling back gender equality to pre-1960s standards
  - This rhetoric frames women's equality as taking "purpose out of the man's life"
  - Similar arguments have historically been used against Black voting rights after the Civil War
  - These arguments portray public benefits as taking resources from "deserving" white men to give to "undeserving" others
  - This rhetoric extends from racial arguments to opposition to women's rights
