## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: World War II transformed American Jewish identity by institutionalizing the "Judeo-Christian tradition" concept, which helped American Jews harmonize their Jewish and American identities by positioning them as equal partners with Christians in American society.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - The wartime military institutionalized the Judeo-Christian tradition as "standard operating procedure"
  - This new framework provided Jews a way to reconcile American and Jewish identities
  - Jewish chaplains played a crucial role in fostering this concept within the military
  - The Judeo-Christian framework emphasized shared democratic values while sustaining religious differences
  - This new identity allowed Jews to assert an identity not rooted in historical oppression
- **Methodology**: Historical analysis of primary sources with theoretical interpretation of identity formation
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to scholarship on American Jewish identity, religious pluralism, and civil religion
