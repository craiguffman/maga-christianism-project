## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: This Wikipedia entry summarizes the academic work of Eric Kaufmann, focusing on his analyses of demographic change, white identity politics, and nationalism, particularly his books "Rise and Fall of Anglo-America" (2004) and "Whiteshift" (2018).
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - Kaufmann analyzes how the Anglo-Protestant dominant group in America lost its status due to its own values of expressive individualism and egalitarianism
  - His book "Whiteshift" argues that demographic changes in Western countries are driving populist reactions, as white majorities respond to their declining majority status
  - He critiques what he calls "left modernism" (political correctness) and "asymmetrical multiculturalism" for preventing mainstream politics from addressing demographic concerns
  - He proposes that the political divide in Western countries revolves around those who want to accelerate versus those who want to slow demographic transformation
  - His work has been both praised for addressing difficult topics and criticized for methodological issues and omissions
- **Methodology**: Wikipedia entry summarizing academic work in political science and demographic analysis
- **Contextual Placement**: Provides context for understanding academic perspectives on white identity politics and demographic change
