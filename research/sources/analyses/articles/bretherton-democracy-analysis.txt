## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: Democracy as both a social practice and system of government has deep connections to Christian belief and practice, creating a synergistic relationship where faithful Christianity generates democratic practices while democracy needs something like Christianity to flourish.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  1. Democracy embodies and enshrines core elements of Christian belief and practice
  2. Biblical warrants exist for democratic social practices and governance
  3. Democracy doesn't require Christianity but benefits from it, while Christianity is best practiced through democratic forms
  4. Democratic politics requires virtue, care, and moral vision to avoid becoming "pitiless"
  5. Democratic politics properly conceived is a work of neighbor love
- **Methodology**: Theological analysis exploring the relationship between Christian theology and democratic theory
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to political theology discourse on the relationship between Christianity and democracy
