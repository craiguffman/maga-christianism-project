## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: A "moral perfection narrative" offers a potentially unifying alternative to Trump's decline narrative, drawing from religious and civic sources to frame America as an unfinished moral project.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - Americans are divided by competing narratives about national identity and trajectory
  - Democrats in 2016 were split between multiculturalist progress and revolutionary change narratives
  - The moral perfection narrative presents America as an unfinished moral project requiring ongoing work
  - This narrative, associated with figures like Obama and Rev. William Barber II, combines elements of progress and critique
  - Progressive religious leaders are key carriers of this narrative tradition
- **Methodology**: Narrative analysis of political discourse with historical contextualization
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to discussions about the role of religion in progressive politics and national identity formation
