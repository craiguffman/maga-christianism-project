## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: The term "Bourbon Democrat" emerged in 1871 to describe white southern Democrats who refused to accept Reconstruction amendments and Black voting rights, drawing on symbolic parallels to the French royal family that clung to tradition after revolution.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - The term "Bourbon Democrat" was first used in 1871 to describe reactionary white Democrats
  - Bourbon Democrats opposed the Fourteenth and Fifteenth Amendments that guaranteed civil rights
  - The name referenced the French royal family that refused to change after revolution
  - These Democrats framed their opposition to Black voting in terms of class rather than race
  - The "Bourbon" label became a symbol of the Old South and Confederate loyalty
- **Methodology**: Historical analysis using primary sources and newspapers
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to understanding post-Civil War racial politics and the evolution of the Democratic Party
