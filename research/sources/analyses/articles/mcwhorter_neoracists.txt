## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: A new "Third Wave Antiracism" has emerged that differs fundamentally from previous antiracist movements by teaching that racism is structurally embedded in society, making whites inherently complicit and reshaping standards for Black Americans.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - Antiracism has evolved through three distinct historical waves
  - First Wave Antiracism fought slavery and segregation
  - Second Wave Antiracism in the 1970s-80s focused on changing racist attitudes
  - Third Wave Antiracism (2010s) views racism as baked into societal structures
  - This newest wave requires different standards for different racial groups
- **Methodology**: Historical periodization with conceptual analysis
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to contemporary debates about antiracism, critical race theory, and racial discourse
