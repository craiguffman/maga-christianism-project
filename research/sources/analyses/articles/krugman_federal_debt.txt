## 2. Core Analysis
- **Central Thesis**: While federal debt matters, the significant increases in US debt since 2007 were largely justified responses to economic and public health emergencies, and we should work to reduce deficits now during full employment but face political obstacles to doing so.
- **Key Arguments**: 
  - Deficits increase spending in the economy, which is beneficial during economic downturns but can be problematic in full employment
  - What matters is debt as a percentage of GDP, not absolute debt levels
  - Running deficits during crises (2008 financial crisis, COVID-19 pandemic) was economically necessary
  - The current political situation makes deficit reduction difficult despite it being economically appropriate now
  - The federal government functions primarily as "an insurance company with an army" with most spending in defense and social programs
- **Methodology**: Economic analysis with historical context and policy implications
- **Contextual Placement**: Contributes to public understanding of federal debt dynamics at a time of high debt levels and political debates about fiscal policy
