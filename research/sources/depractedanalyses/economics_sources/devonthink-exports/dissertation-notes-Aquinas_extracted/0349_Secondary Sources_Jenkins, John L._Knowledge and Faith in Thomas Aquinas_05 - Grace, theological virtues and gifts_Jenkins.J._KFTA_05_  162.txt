# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  162

As characterized so far, any and every creaturely good is a grace2 which proceeds from divine grace,. Aquinas, however, draws an important distinction between two sorts of divine love on the basis of two sorts of creaturely good: "One [love is] common, by which `[God] loves all things that exist' (Wis. 11:24) ... and in accord with it natural existence is bestowed on creatures.
