# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  200

This, then, is the fourth difference between the merely human and the theological virtues. The former are such as to be directed by the light of natural reason, and natural reason directs toward the end which corresponds to UNO nature. The latter are such as to be directed by a "higher" light, the lumen gratiae, and this directs one toward the higher end of perfect beatitude, that end which corresponds to the "higher" nature which is a participation in the divine nature.
