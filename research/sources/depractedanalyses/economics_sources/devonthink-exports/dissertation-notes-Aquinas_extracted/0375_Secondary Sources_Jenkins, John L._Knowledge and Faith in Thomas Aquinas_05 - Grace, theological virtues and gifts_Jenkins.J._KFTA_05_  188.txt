# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  188

The theological virtues are infused active principles insofar as they are dispositions for self-moved actions. And though they are directed toward a higher object (viz. God in Himself) than the merely human virtues, they are less perfectly possessed: "for [a theological virtue] is possessed as something imperfect; for we imperfectly know and love God."81 In the case of faith, for example, it is apprehension of God as He is in Himself, and thus is superior to natural knowledge of God through His effects; but it is imperfectly possessed by us in this life, for it does not include a full grasp of the divine essence.Read more at location 2153
