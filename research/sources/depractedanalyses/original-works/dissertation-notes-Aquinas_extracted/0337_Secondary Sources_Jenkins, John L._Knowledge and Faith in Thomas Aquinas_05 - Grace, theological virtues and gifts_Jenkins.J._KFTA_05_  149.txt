# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  149

In summary, God can move creatures outside the UNO as He wills. Though such movement is above the UNO (and thus supra naturam), it is not against nature simpliciter (and thus not contra naturam), for it is in accord with a prior and more fundamental natural order, the MUNO, the order of all creatures to the divine will. All creatures were created with an intrinsic, formal, passive potency to obey God's will, and this is what Aquinas calls an obediential potency. But to say that there are such obediential potencies, and that God can move creatures supra naturam, is not to say that such movements occur, or what sorts of movements occur.Read more at location 1988
