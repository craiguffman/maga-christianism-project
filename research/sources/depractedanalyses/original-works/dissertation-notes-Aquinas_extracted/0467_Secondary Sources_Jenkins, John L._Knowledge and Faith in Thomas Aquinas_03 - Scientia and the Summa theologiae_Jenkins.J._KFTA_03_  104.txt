# Jenkins.J._KFTA_03:  104

it is possible that when Aquinas says that he is writing the Summa theologiae for beginners rather than for the advanced, the presupposed contrast is not between advanced students and rank beginners; it is between students, albeit very advanced students, on the one hand, and Aquinas's fellow theological Masters at Paris, on the other. The former group would have been very accomplished, would have been studying for a long time and would not have been far from their inception as Masters.
