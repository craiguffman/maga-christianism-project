The goal of inquiry, then, is to acquire such a familiarity with the causes in the field under study that the effects come to be not surprising but expected on the basis of our apprehension of the causes. It is not enough simply to reason to the cause, for immediately upon discovery of it the cause may still be less well known than its effects, and even inferred from these effects.
