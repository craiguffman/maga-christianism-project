# Jenkins.J._KFTA_05:  153

The good which a human being may attain through the proper exercise of his strictly natural potencies is what Aquinas calls imperfect beatitude. Since imperfect beatitude is attainable through the exercise of potencies one has simply in virtue of having a human nature, it is said to be "proportionate" to human nature: "One beatitude is proportionate to human nature, which a human can of course attain through the principles of his nature."47Read more at location 2014
