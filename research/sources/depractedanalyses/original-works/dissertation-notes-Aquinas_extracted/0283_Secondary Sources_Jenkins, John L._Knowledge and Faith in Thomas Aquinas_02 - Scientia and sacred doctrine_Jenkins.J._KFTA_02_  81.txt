Sacred doctrine therefore has what we might call a `mixed' character, in that it is a participation in a higher kind of scientia by a lower kind of cognitive being. And it is this mixed character of sacred doctrine that distinguishes it from other human scientiae and gives it its distinctive features. As we shall see, some truths of sacred doctrine are of singular things and events of the sort which cannot be known within a merely human scientia, but can be part of this scientia in which the human individual relies upon God's cognitive powers. Thus this human scientia has much in common with the higher scientiae of God and angels, and in these respects is superior to merely human scientiae.Read more at location 943
