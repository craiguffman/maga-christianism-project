I am arguing, then, that the Summa was written for these extremely well prepared and highly capable students who studied, and eventually commented upon, the Sentences of Peter Lombard at the University of Paris. These students could have been expected to handle the dense, difficult and copious material presented in that work. Moreover, since our best evidence is that the Summa theologiae was written to play a role similar to that which the Sentences of Peter Lombard played, it is most likely that it was written for the students who were at the level at which the Lombard's work was to be studied.
