# Jenkins.J._KFTA_02:  62

Scientia, as was said in chapter one, is an epistemic virtue or perfection. The PA recognized that various scientiae, various kinds of epistemic virtues, could be distinguished with respect to their subject matter, their subject genus. Geometry is distinct from arithmetic because the former concerns the genus of spatial magnitude while the latter concerns the genus of number.
