# Walsh.L._TheologyAquinas_Sacraments:  642

To be sanctified by Christ is not only to receive the remission of sin; it is to be joined to him in worship.  It is to be made able to give oneself to the Father in him.  Aquinas gives full Pauline realism to this joining with Christ:  “The sacraments of the Church take their power especially from the passion of Christ, whose power is somehow joined to us by the reception of sacraments [the Latin has the grossly realistic copulatur, which must be reminiscent of Paul’s sexual language about membership of Christ in 1 Cor 6] p. 348
